Ngoro Ngoro Crater

(13 reviews)

The Ngorongoro Crater, formed by a massive volcanic collapse, is a stunning natural wonder, spanning 304 sq. km.

Home to diverse wildlife, including lions, rhinos, and more, it offers a breathtaking view from its 610m high rim.

The crater's unique beauty defies comparison, making it a true wonder of the world. Expect chilly evenings, especially from May to September, at this high-altitude location.

Tarangire National Park

(7 reviews)

Tarangire National Park's vast terrain includes grassy savannahs, extensive swamps, and the meandering Tarangire River, all framed by ancient baobab trees.

Renowned for its rich birdlife, the park also offers exceptional game viewing. Elephants are the highlight, often seen in large herds along the riverbanks.

The park's size allows for crowd-free exploration and abundant wildlife encounters, including a diverse range of ungulates, big cats, primates, and even tree-climbing pythons.

Lake Manyara National Park

(9 reviews)

Located beside the Rift Valley escarpment, Lake Manyara National Park offers spectacular sunrise and sunset views, making it a uniquely scenic destination in Tanzania

Its lush green forests stand in contrast to the savannah-dominated parks, and it's renowned for tree-climbing lions, elephants, buffalo, and flamingos.

With over 400 bird species, it's a paradise for ornithologists, featuring pelicans, spoonbills, Egyptian geese, and the stunning sight of migratory flamingoes."
